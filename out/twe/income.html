What counts as Social Security income?

Social security is benefit payments made to retirees, people with disabilities, and their families through the Social Security Administration (SSA). To report this income at the end of the year, the SSA sends Form SSA-1099.

Don’t include Supplemental Security Income (SSI) payments. The estimator doesn’t currently support SSI payments.

What’s the monthly benefit amount and where do I find it?

The monthly benefit amount is the total you receive from Social Security each month before any deductions, Medicare premiums, or taxes are taken out.

It’s okay to estimate. To find the exact amount:

  1. Log into my Social Security account
  2. Scroll to the Benefits and Payments section
  3. Under Benefit Summary, use the total monthly benefit before deductions

If you need help finding the monthly benefit amount, call the Social Security Administration at 1-800-772-1213.

How do I know if I have taxes withhheld?

The Social Security Administration doesn’t automatically withhold federal taxes from your Social Security income.

You can request to withhold federal taxes from your Social Security income.

How to check if you have taxes withheld:

  1. Log into my Social Security account
  2. Select Voluntary Tax Withholding to check the percentage withheld

If you didn’t request to withhold taxes, the percentage will be 0%.

If you don’t have a my Social Security account, or need help checking your federal withholdings, call the Social Security Administration at 1-800-772-1213.
